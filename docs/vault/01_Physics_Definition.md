# Physics Definition

## Gravitational Lensing Basics
A gravitational lens produces multiple images of a background source when the source lies near a caustic of the lens potential. Quad lenses (4-image systems) are particularly valuable because their flux ratios encode information about small-scale structure along the line of sight.

## Flux-Ratio Anomalies
In a smooth lens potential, the magnification ratios between images follow predictable patterns (the fold relation, cusp relation, etc.). Deviations from these patterns — "flux-ratio anomalies" — are interpreted as evidence for dark matter substructure.

## Substructure Perturbations
Subhalos (bound dark matter clumps within the lens halo) and LOS halos (structures along the line of sight but not bound to the lens) each perturb the lens potential on small scales, breaking the symmetry of the image configuration and causing flux-ratio anomalies.

## Observable Definition
R_min quantifies the minimum flux asymmetry among image pairs at similar radii from the lens centroid. This is designed to capture fold-pair-like asymmetries without needing a lens model.

## Source of Anomalies
- Subhalos in the lens plane (mass function dN/dm ∝ m^-1.9, 10^6-10^9 Msun)
- LOS halos (line-of-sight structure)
- Macro-model deviations (ellipticity, shear, multipoles)
- Measurement noise + systematic contaminants (microlensing, dust)
